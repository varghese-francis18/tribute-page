THE RUN MACHINE

About

Virat Kohli is an Indian cricketer and former captain of the Indian national cricket team. He was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India. Kohli is widely regarded as one of the best batsmen in the world and has numerous records to his name. Kohli made his debut for the Indian cricket team in 2008 and has since become a mainstay in the team's batting lineup. He has scored more than 12,000 runs in One Day Internationals (ODIs) and more than 7,500 runs in Test cricket. He has also scored 70 international centuries, making him one of the greatest batsmen in the history of the sport. Kohli has received numerous awards for his performances, including the Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year in 2017 and 2018. He has also been named the ICC ODI Player of the Year three times. In addition to his cricketing achievements, Kohli is known for his philanthropy work and is involved in various charitable organizations. He is also a successful entrepreneur, with his own brand of fitness clothing and a chain of gyms. Off the field, Kohli is known for his passion and intensity. He is a fitness enthusiast and follows a strict workout routine to stay in top shape. Kohli is also involved in various charitable organizations and has his own foundation called the Virat Kohli Foundation, which focuses on supporting underprivileged children and promoting sports in India. In personal life, Kohli is married to Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma and the couple has a daughter named Vamika.

Timeline

  • 1988 November 5-Virat Kohli was born on 5 November 1988 in Delhi into a Punjabi Khatri family. His father, Prem Kohli, worked as a criminal lawyer and his mother, Saroj Kohli, is a housewife.
  • 1998- the West Delhi Cricket Academy was created, and Kohli, a nine-year-old, was part of its first intake. Kohli's father took him to the academy
  • 2002- Kohli first played for Delhi Under-15 team in October 2002 in the 2002–03 Polly Umrigar Trophy. He was the leading run-scorer for his team in that tournament with 172 runs at an average of 34.40.
  • 2006 October- Kohli was selected in the India Under-19 squad on its tour of England. He averaged 105 in the three-match ODI series against England Under-19s and 49 in the three-match Test series. India Under-19 went on to win both the series.
  • 2006 November-Kohli made his first-class debut for Delhi against Tamil Nadu in November 2006, at the age of 18, and scored 10 in his debut innings. He came into the spotlight in December when he decided to play for his team against Karnataka on the day after his father's death and went on to score 90. He went directly to the funeral after he was dismissed. Delhi captain Mithun Manhas said, "That is an act of great commitment to the team and his innings turned out to be crucial," while coach Chetan Chauhan lauded Kohli's "attitude and determination."
  • 2007- he made his Twenty20 debut and finished as the highest run-getter for his team in the Inter-State T20 Championship with 179 runs at an average of 35.80. In July–August 2007, the India Under-19 team toured Sri Lanka.
  • 2008 February- Kohli captained the victorious Indian team at the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup held in Malaysia. Batting at number 3, he scored 235 runs in 6 matches at an average of 47 and finished as the tournament's third-highest run-getter and one of the three batsmen to score a hundred in the tournament. His century (100 runs from 74 balls) against the West Indies Under-19s in the group stage, which was called "the innings of the tournament" by ESPNcricinfo, gave India a 50-run victory and earned Kohli the man of the match.
  • 2008 March- Kohli was bought on a youth contract by the Indian Premier League franchise Royal Challengers Bangalore for $30,000.
  • 2008 August- Kohli was included in the Indian ODI squad for tour of Sri Lanka and the Champions Trophy in Pakistan.
  • 2008 November- Kohli was included in the squad for the home ODI series against England in November 2008 but was not given a chance to play
  • 2009-Kohli returned to the national team replacing the injured Gautam Gambhir in the Indian squad for the tri-series in Sri Lanka. He batted at number 4 for India in the 2009 ICC Champions Trophy because of an injury to Yuvraj Singh. In the inconsequential group match against the West Indies, Kohli scored an unbeaten 79 in India's successful chase of 130 and won his first man of the match award.
  • 2010-Despite the poor run of form, Kohli was retained in the ODI squad for a three-match series against Australia in October, and in the only completed match of the series at Visakhapatnam, scored his third ODI century–118 off 121 balls–which helped India reach the target of 290 after losing the openers early.
  • 2011-Despite the poor run of form, Kohli was retained in the ODI squad for a three-match series against Australia in October, and in the only completed match of the series at Visakhapatnam, scored his third ODI century–118 off 121 balls–which helped India reach the target of 290 after losing the openers early.
  • 2011 December-In the fourth and final match at Adelaide, Kohli scored his maiden Test century of 116 runs in the first innings; it was the only century scored by an Indian in the series. India suffered a 0–4 whitewash and Kohli, India's top run-scorer in the series, was described as "the lone bright spot in an otherwise nightmare visit for the tourists".
  • 2012-ICC ODI Player of the Year
  • 2013-No 1 batsman in ODI rankings
  • 2014-Appointed Test captain,Man of tournament ICC t20 worldcup
  • 2017 January- Appointed as the ODI captain
  • 2017-ICC Cricketer of the Year
  • 2018-Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna
  • 2018: Kohli leads India to their first-ever Test series win in Australia.
  • 2019: Kohli becomes the fastest player to reach 11,000 runs in ODI cricket.
  • 2020: Kohli takes a paternity leave during India's tour of Australia to be with his wife Anushka Sharma for the birth of their daughter.
  • 2021: Kohli steps down as the captain of India's T20I team, but continues to lead the Test and ODI teams. He also becomes the first cricketer from India to hit 100 million followers on Instagram.
